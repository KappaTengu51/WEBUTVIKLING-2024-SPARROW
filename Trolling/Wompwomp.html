Who is Jesus?

Jesus is a central figure in Christianity, believed to be the Son of God and the Messiah prophesied in the Old Testament. Born in Bethlehem around 4-6 B.C., he lived a life of teaching, preaching love, forgiveness, and salvation. Jesus performed miracles, healed the sick, and emphasized the importance of loving God and others. He was crucified by the Romans around A.D. 30-33, an event Christians believe was a sacrifice for humanity's sins. According to Christian belief, he rose from the dead three days later, affirming his divine nature and offering eternal life to believers.
